## Market Analysis

### Rwanda Agriculture Overview

**Statistics** (Rwanda National Institute of Statistics, 2023):
- **Total Population**: 13.4 million
- **Agricultural Employment**: 70% of population
- **Farmers**: ~2.5 million active farmers
- **Agricultural GDP**: 24% of total GDP (~$2.8 billion)
- **Smallholder Farms**: 90% of farms are <1 hectare

**Key Crops** (by production):
1. Bananas - 1.2M tons/year
2. Sweet Potatoes - 950K tons/year
3. Cassava - 850K tons/year
4. Maize - 600K tons/year
5. Beans - 450K tons/year
6. Irish Potatoes - 350K tons/year
7. Coffee - 25K tons/year (high-value export)
8. Tea - 32K tons/year (high-value export)

### Market Problems

1. **Crop Disease Losses**: 
   - 30-40% annual crop losses due to diseases
   - Limited access to agricultural extension services
   - Lack of timely disease diagnosis

2. **Market Information Gap**:
   - Farmers sell at unfair prices due to information asymmetry
   - No real-time price transparency
   - Middlemen exploit farmers

3. **Poor Farm Management**:
   - Manual record-keeping or none at all
   - No data-driven decision making
   - Inefficient resource allocation

4. **Limited Market Access**:
   - Farmers struggle to find buyers
   - High post-harvest losses (up to 35%)
   - No direct connection to large buyers

5. **Weather Unpredictability**:
   - Climate change affecting traditional farming patterns
   - No access to localized weather forecasts
   - Poor irrigation planning

**Total Addressable Market (TAM)**:
- 2.5M farmers in Rwanda

- **TAM**: $50 million/year (Rwanda only)

**Serviceable Available Market (SAM)**:

### 2. Marketplace Commission (Secondary Revenue)

**Model**: Take a percentage of each successful transaction

**Commission Structure**:
- **Free users**: 2% commission
- **Basic users**: 1% commission
- **Premium users**: 0% commission (incentive to upgrade)
- **Enterprise**: Custom rates

**Example**:
- Farmer sells 1000 kg maize @ 500 RWF/kg = 500,000 RWF
- Commission (2%): 10,000 RWF
- Farmer nets: 490,000 RWF (still better than middlemen who take 20-30%)
